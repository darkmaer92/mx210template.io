Has the time come?

Industrial Ethernet has kept surpassing traditional fieldbus architectures year on year since 20181, and thus, becomes the leading connection methodology in plants around the world. Since the Industrial Ethernet is now an up-and-coming star on the industrial automation stage, we introduce remote Ethernet I/O Modules based on Modbus TCP.
It’s time to stop lagging behind and become a part of the trend, together with akYtec and Mx210 Series I/O Modules.

1 according to HMS Networks, an international company in the field of industrial communications

Why Modbus TCP?

Modbus TCP is an open, globally accepted, simple, vendor-neutral communication protocol. Being on the top 4 of industrial ethernet protocols, Modbus TCP is a ‘de-facto’ automation standard. If your system is not deterministic and doesn’t require high-level real-time performance, it’s not reasonable to employ high-end ethernet protocols, such as EtherCat, Profinet, Ethernet/IP. Why would you pay more if Modbus TCP is enough for your task?

Remote I/O modules Mx210 come in useful if you need:

Extend Migrate Integrate Connect

Flexibly extend existing I/Os
of a PLC-based system

The Ethernet I/O-Modules of the MX210 series are available in different variants depending on the combination of the number, type, and properties of their inputs and outputs.
The analog inputs and outputs of the respective device variant are individually configurable to several signal types, which adds to the flexibility of your system even more. You can create your own I/O-type combinations within a single device to match the requirements of the task.

Migrate from Fieldbus to Industrial Ethernet
or create a PLC-based system on Industrial Ethernet

As compared to fieldbus-based networks, industrial Ethernet communications are much faster, have greater bandwidth, easier upward integration, unlimited node counts, and more topology options. Also, Ethernet networks are far easier to deploy and use than fieldbus technologies.

Smoothly integrate
into IIoT infrastructure

Thanks to the MQTT communication protocol, an Mx210 module can connect to any MQTT brokers providing integration between factory installations and IT-systems / IIoT applications, and thus a range of possibilities, such as remote monitoring and control via any browser-enabled devices, PCs, smartphones, tablets, etc.

What modules we offer:

  • Analog input modules
    (MV210-101)

    8 individually configurable analog inputs to connect a wide range of sensors, such as RTDs (Pt100/1000, Ni100/1000), TCs, and signals 0-1 V, 0(4)-20 mA.

  • Digital input modules
    (MV210-202/204/212/214)

    Up to 32 inputs to connect switch contacts as well as NPN or PNP transistors. High-speed counting with 100-KHz inputs to connect encoders or pulse counters.

  • Digital input module
    (MV210-221)

    • - 6 input to connect switch contacts as well as NPN transistors (support for 400-Hz-counting)
    • - 9 digital inputs for connecting 230 V AC signals for:
      • determination of the presence or absence of voltage in the network
      • phase break diagnostics in a three-phase network
      • phase rotation control
      • voltage switching counter
      • other needs

Digital input/output modules
(MK210-311/312)

Convenient combination of inputs and outputs:
  • - 6 or 12 digital inputs implemented to be connected to switch contacts or NPN/PNP sensors. No power required to supply the inputs
  • - 8 or 4 digital outputs (relay)

Digital output modules
(MU210-401/402/410)

8 or 16 digital outputs (relay or transistor outputs). The maximum current load is 5 A at 250 V AC or 3A at 24 V DC. Support for pulse-width modulation (PWM) for PID control, etc.

Analog output module
(MU210-501)

8 analog individually configurable outputs: 0(4)-20 mA, 0-1(10)V. Support for pulse-width modulation (PWM) for PID control, etc. Configurable output signal update rate.
  • USB-powered in the configuration mode
    USB-powered
  • This feature saves commissioning time and enables making similar setups cost-effectively.
    Easy mass deployment
  • Thanks to the support of the SNTP protocol, the built-in real-time clock is synchronized over the network
    Built-in real-time clock
  • Each module has FLASH memory for logging. In case of a communication loss, it is possible to recover the missing data.
    Support for data logging
